Project 1 â€” Friendly Campus for Atlantis Underwater Visitors

HKUST will host visiting mermaid students from Atlantis. We need to adapt our land campus to their body, habits, and culture. This page introduces the project and links to my diary and reflection.

Description & Goal

POV: A mermaid exchange student needs waterâ€‘based infrastructure and learning tools. Their anatomy and habits do not fit a dry, stairâ€‘filled, screenâ€‘based campus. They should be able to study well and live comfortably in HK. They should also be able to order food easily at cafeteria kiosks.

Needs & Obstacles

  • Mobility: Escalators, stairs, and long paths are hard for mermaids. They need water slides, swim tubes, and water elevators so they can move without leaving water.
  • Academic: Paper and normal electronics get wet and stop working. They need waterproof devices and materials so they can study and take notes.
  • Health: Dry air dehydrates them. They need easy access to seawater hydration stations and humid spaces. For long dry routes, wearable water enclosures could help them stay comfortable.
  • Social: Food and clubs are mostly designed for land students. They often eat fish and seaweed, so more aquatic cuisine and inclusive activities would help them join campus life.

Project Goal: Design campus policies, infrastructure, and tools so mermaid students can study, socialize, and live well. We will start with cafeteria kiosks: make the ordering flow easy with wet hands, using a waterproof stylus and bigger targets.
